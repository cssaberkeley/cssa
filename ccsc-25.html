    CCSC 2024

    The California Cognitive Science Conference at UC Berkeley is an annual all-day symposium bringing together hundreds of students, researchers, and members of the general public from around the world who are passionate about the interdisciplinary field of Cognitive Science for a day of presentations and research presentations. We feature talks given by prominent scientists and thinkers from a wide variety of disciplines, and our acclaimed poster session provides undergraduates with the opportunity to present their original research alongside graduate students and professional researchers. The theme for this year's annual conference is "Pathways to Purpose: The Cognitive Pursuit of Clarity." Through this event, our speakers will explore perspectives on human purpose amidst the dynamic landscape of our modern world. The conference provides attendees with a glimpse into the latest research in all the fields that comprise Cognitive Science, including but not limited to Psychology, Neuroscience, Computer Science/Artificial Intelligence, Linguistics, Philosophy, and Sociology.

    Teaching Fellow

    Yoona Kong (she/her) is an Assistant Professor of Psychology at Rutgers University–Camden. Her research explores how social experiences impact health, focusing on evidence-based strategies like compassion, purpose, and mindfulness. She uses multimethod approaches—combining neuroimaging, behavioral experiments, social network analysis, and natural language processing—to study wellness topics such as physical activity, alcohol use, and loneliness. Kong holds a B.A. from UCLA and a Ph.D. from Yale, and previously conducted research at the University of Pennsylvania’s Annenberg School for Communication.
